# Ball-Pit-Physics-Simulator

<img width="841" height="660" alt="BPPS" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/40924277-c5a2-4302-814e-d192368ad973" />

A physics simulator that simulates balls (sort of) accurately with customizable values such as restitution, friction, and many more.  
I made this for fun, not for personal use, not for YOUR use. I made it just to challenge myself and do something new.

## First things first

I started by making Euler integration and wall collision.  
Then, I added ball collision (which I implemented wrong and didn't realize until the next day).

## A completely different direction

I then shifted the entire thing onto another script that handled everything from a single object and made it spawn balls.

## Optimizing to the death

I wanted to optimize my simulator so badly after seeing [this video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9IULfQH7E90&t=192s) by [Pezzza's Work](https://www.youtube.com/@PezzzasWork).

So I started with the technique that he mentioned in the video. After that, I made a lot more tiny optimizations and some more major ones like sleeping agents, which took me three days to tackle.

## The end and the realization

After all of that, I was done. I made an [itch.io page](https://cof99.itch.io/ball-pit-physcics-s).

But then I realized that the real physics simulator is the friends we made along the way.
